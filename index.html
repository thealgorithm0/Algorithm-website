Community Definition

The Algorithm, a student-founded and student-led tech community at Padma Kanya Campus, is dedicated to leveraging technology for the betterment of society and gender equality. Representation of females in STEM is very low, primarily due to a lack of instilled confidence, access to learning STEM concepts, role models, and more. One common sentiment among the females in my university is that we are limiting ourselves due to a lack of confidence and imposter syndrome. Therefore, The Algorithm community focuses on helping females build confidence.

Senior year university students organize workshops and events for junior year students. As the junior students gain knowledge, the senior students, serving as organizers or mentors, also gain confidence by exposing themselves to leadership roles. In the past 10 months, our community has expanded beyond its initial scope. Initially, we had 8 executive members, but now we have added 3 more to our board. Additionally, we have positively impacted over 500 women through our events, workshops, and projects. We have expanded our reach by collaborating with prominent organizations.

One of our significant milestones is being included in the Women in Data Steering Committee by The Asia Foundation, where we work on dismantling systemic barriers and fostering a more equitable future for women in the data and technology landscape. Currently, we are focusing on open data and how it can be leveraged to make data-driven policies. To this end, we are organizing hackathons outside the capital city, focusing on these themes. Together, we pave the way for a brighter, more inclusive tomorrow.

Shared Struggles

  • Lack of Confidence and Imposter Syndrome: Many of us struggle with self-doubt and imposter syndrome, which can limit our potential.
  • Desire for Leadership Skills: We seek opportunities to practice and develop our leadership skills.
  • Volunteering Experience: Gaining volunteering experience is important to us, as it helps in securing internships and building our resumes.
  • Helping Newbies in Tech: We are passionate about supporting and mentoring newcomers in the tech field.

Mission

Our community's mission is to build an ecosystem within the campus where everyone feels welcome to practice their leadership skills. The heart of our community is that senior year students organize workshops for junior year students. This approach allows senior students to lead events, boosting their confidence, while junior students benefit from the knowledge and experience of their seniors. An additional benefit for senior year students is that these leadership experiences enhance their resumes, making it easier to secure internships.
